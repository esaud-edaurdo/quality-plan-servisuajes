Objective & Scope

Objective

To ensure that the processes of each department are standardized and suitable for their manufacturing processes, creating tools for the validation of each process, and ensuring that all manufactured products meet functional and technical requirements.

Scope

This plan applies to the entire product development life cycle, from the receipt of the manufacturing request to the final delivery of the product, including the following processes:

  • Reception of manufacturing request.
  • Response time for quotations and documentation.
  • Product manufacturing planning.
  • Process of obtaining raw materials and components.
  • Design development.
  • Laser cutting.
  • CNC machining process for counter-plates.
  • Component machining (if applicable).
  • Die and/or tooling assembly process.
  • Ejection rubber cutting and assembly process.
  • Packaging and final product shipment process.

Departmental Responsibilities

Customer Service & Planning

Customer Service: Responsible for receiving all information (technical, administrative, financial) from clients. Must ensure that personnel capture all obtained information in the system and supervise the fulfillment of their responsibilities.

  • Create manufacturing requests.
  • Obtain all information for product elaboration.
  • Provide financial information to accounting.
  • Capture all information in the system.
  • Notify planning about the request.

Planning: In charge of planning the manufacturing process, supplementing the request with technical data of the die (material, machine, stripping, observations, measurements, components, tolerances, base, cutting rules, dates, drawings, etc.). Must ensure the Work Order (WO) contains all necessary information for production.

Accounting & Finance

Accounting: Responsible for the company's accounting, ensuring the integrity, accuracy, and traceability of accounting records and compliance with fiscal and accounting regulations.

Finance: Guarantees quality in the preparation of budgets, financial analysis, and strategic reports, ensuring data consistency for decision-making.

Purchasing/Warehouse

Ensure the warehouse has all components and raw materials for manufacturing. Each component must meet the quality requirements of the technical sheets. Must ensure that suppliers comply with the imposed requirements, choosing the best quality-price option, and that stock is always covered.

Engineering

Responsible for the design, functionality, technical integrity of the product, and compliance with the quality standards of the client and the company. The department head must ensure that each designed product complies with regulations, continuously reviewing each manufactured product.

Laser Cutting

Each product must leave with the proper conditions for the next process. The personnel are responsible for meeting quality standards by making necessary adjustments to parameters. The head must train the staff and supervise that each product meets the client's requirements.

Machining

Ensure the dimensional quality of each product and the correct assembly, performing a detailed inspection of the drawings. The department head must ensure that the personnel have the necessary training for machinery operation, tools, and assembly.

Production

Responsible for performing the proper assemblies, ensuring compliance with standards, managing time, and controlling defects. The head must ensure that the personnel are trained and that each product complies with: correct assembly, dimensional quality, machine handling, and times. Must maintain control of waste and defects.

Ejection Rubber Assembly (Rubbering)

Responsible for placing the appropriate ejection rubber on each product, adhering to the manufacturing request. The personnel must be able to design, operate cutting machines, and apply the rubber. The head must ensure training in software and machines.

Logistics

Responsible for ensuring and verifying packaging, traceability, and delivery conditions. All vehicles must be inspected. The head must verify that the transportation is adequate and plan deliveries with the Planning department.

Maintenance

Perform preventive, corrective, and predictive maintenance to ensure machines are in optimal condition. Status reports for each machine must be created. The head must ensure that the personnel have the proper training and technical knowledge.

Joint Responsibilities

All departments must complete the product validation documentation (check-list). All heads must ensure and train their personnel in its completion. Additionally, they must create the necessary reports for manufactured products (components, wood, cutting rule, observations, etc.).

Quality Requirements

To ensure that products, processes, and services comply with technical, regulatory, and customer satisfaction standards.

Normative & Regulatory Requirements

ISO 9001:2015: Follow, update, and verify compliance with the standard, applying the PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycle.

Sector Regulation: Compliance with specific technical regulations, product safety, labeling, traceability, and environmental and labor standards (SEMARNAT, STPS).

Legal: Current licenses, permits, and tax registrations. Compliance with occupational health, consumer protection, and civil liability regulations.

Technical Requirements

Documentation for engineering and planning that specifies each design in detail (drawings, tolerances, materials). Establish standards for process parameters (laser cutting, machining, etc.). Implement quality control processes for receiving materials from suppliers.

Operational Requirements

Verify the complete filling of documentation at each stage (WO, check-list, reports). Generate virtual traceability reports. Verify that production cycle times are met.

Customer Requirements

Create technical manuals for clients with details on design, manufacturing, and delivery. Ensure that quality verification documents contain all technical aspects of the client. The customer satisfaction rate must be >= 85%.

Administrative & Financial Requirements

Accuracy in accounting and financial records. Transparency in budgets, costs, and profitability analysis. Compliance with purchasing, payment, and budget control policies.

Management & Improvement

Non-Conformity Management

All rejected products (internally or externally) will be recorded in a consecutive log, describing the reason and generating a report of expenses, processes, and personnel involved.

Root Cause Analysis Tools:

Ishikawa Diagram 5 Whys Pareto Analysis FTA Analysis

A corrective action will be generated with follow-up on the actions to be implemented and the problem.

Continuous Improvement

Feedback meetings will be held on detected failures, rejected products, process improvement, and innovation. All improvements will be recorded and the corresponding documentation will be updated and distributed to personnel.

Training will be sought in processes, machines, tools, and software. Continuous improvement will be led by the quality department, management, and direction.
